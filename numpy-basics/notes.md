## Variance and Standard Deviation

### Variance
**Variance** is the average of the squared differences between each data point and the mean, showing how spread out the data values are.

### Standard Deviation
**Standard deviation** is the square root of the variance and represents the typical distance of data points from the mean, expressed in the same unit as the original data.

---

## Significance

- Both measure **dispersion** (spread) in a dataset.
- A **low value** indicates data points are close to the mean (more consistent).
- A **high value** indicates data points are widely spread (more variable).
- Standard deviation is more practical than variance because:
  - It is expressed in the same unit as the data.
  - It is easier to interpret.
  - It is widely used in statistics, data science, finance, and research.

---

## Practical Importance

- In **finance** → measures risk and volatility.
- In **experiments** → indicates reliability and consistency.
- In **data analysis** → helps detect outliers and understand distribution patterns.

If the mean represents the center of the data, standard deviation describes how tightly or loosely the data clusters around that center.
